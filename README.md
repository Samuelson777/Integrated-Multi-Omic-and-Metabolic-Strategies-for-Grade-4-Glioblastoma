## üìÑ Abstract

Grade 4 Glioblastoma (GBM), specifically the IDH-wildtype variant, represents a primary challenge in neuro-oncology due to its infiltrative nature and therapy resistance. This research evaluates the clinical synergy between mechanical **Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) disruption** via Low-Intensity Focused Ultrasound (LIFU) and the implementation of **Therapeutic Ketogenic Diet (TKD).** By utilizing the **Glucose-Ketone Index (GKI),** we propose a framework to metabolically sensitize tumor cells to standard-of-care chemoradiation.

## üß¨ Key Research Areas

### 1. Molecular Pathogenesis

We analyze the 2026 WHO classification standards, focusing on:

- **IDH-wildtype vs. IDH-mutant**
- **MGMT Promoter Methylation** status as a chemotherapy predictor.
- **EGFR and TERT** promoter mutations.

### 2. Bypassing the Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)

The BBB filters 98% of small-molecule drugs. This paper explores:
- **LIFU Technology:** Mechanical permeabilization of capillary junctions.
- **Nanoparticle Delivery:** Receptor-mediated transcytosis.

### 3. Metabolic Conditioning (The GKI Protocol)

Targeting the **Warburg Effect** to deprive tumor cells of glucose-driven ATP.
